About Me

I am currently working as a Battery Degradation Modelling Engineer at Elysia, UK. My research and engineering interests span across theoretical machine learning, neuroscience-inspired computation, and applied robotics for healthcare and energy systems.

I completed my Master’s in Biorobotics at the University of Bristol, where I focused on brain-computer interfaces and soft robotics, following my undergraduate degree in Electronics and Communication Engineering from CUSAT, India.

In past roles, I have developed computational neuroscience models, EEG-based signal classification pipelines, and machine learning approaches for bio-inspired systems.

My Interests

My current focus areas include:

  • Battery ageing, diagnostics, and modelling for real-world applications
  • EEG signal analysis, brain-computer interfaces, and cognitive load estimation
  • Machine learning approaches for bio-inspired robotics and healthcare systems
  • Generative models (GANs) for biomedical data augmentation

Outside of technical work, I am also interested in exploring robotics, photography, and cross-disciplinary applications of AI.
